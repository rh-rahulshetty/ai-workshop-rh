**Indian Premier League (IPL) 2025 Season Overview**

The 18th edition of the Indian Premier League, branded as TATA IPL 2025, has concluded. The tournament ran from March 22 to June 3, 2025, featuring 10 teams competing across 74 matches. Royal Challengers Bengaluru emerged as the champions, securing their first IPL title.

**Key Highlights and Information:**

* **Dates:** March 22, 2025 - June 3, 2025.
* **Champions:** Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB)
* **Player of the Tournament:** Suryakumar Yadav (Mumbai Indians)
* **Most Expensive Players at Auction:**
    * Indian: Rishabh Pant (INR 27 crore to Lucknow Super Giants) - *Note: This made him the most expensive player in IPL history.*
    * Overseas: Jos Buttler (INR 15.75 crore to Gujarat Titans)
* **Most Expensive Uncapped Player:** Rasikh Dar (INR 6 crore to Royal Challengers Bengaluru)

**Tournament Structure:**

* **Format:** Group stage followed by playoffs (Qualifier 1, Eliminator, Qualifier 2, and Final).
* **Teams:** 10 franchises participated.

**Key Rule Changes and Continuations for IPL 2025:**

* **Saliva Ban Lifted:** Bowlers were once again permitted to use saliva on the ball, a reversal of the COVID-19 era restriction.
* **'Second Ball' Rule:** To counter dew effects in night matches, the bowling team had the option to request a new ball after the 10th over of the second innings if excessive dew was detected. Umpires retained the authority to change the ball earlier if deemed too wet or damaged.
* **Impact Player Rule:** Continued from previous seasons, allowing teams to substitute one player during a match.
* **DRS Expansion:** The Decision Review System (DRS) was expanded to include referrals for height-based no-balls and wide-ball reviews outside the off-stump, utilizing Hawk-Eye technology.
* **New Code of Conduct:** A demerit points system and suspension points were introduced, valid for 36 months.
* **Temporary Replacements:** For the final leg of the season, a special rule allowed temporary replacement players to be signed in case of injury or sickness, even if a team had played more than 12 games. These temporary signings were not eligible for retention.

**Squads (General Overview - specific players can be queried):**

The IPL 2025 mega auction saw significant changes to team compositions. Each of the 10 franchises had an auction purse of INR 120 crore and were allowed to retain a maximum of six players (max 5 capped, 2 uncapped). Notable retentions and auction buys shaped the new look of all teams.
